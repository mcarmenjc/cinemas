  • Thursday 18th Feb

  • A Bigger Splash [15]

    2016, 124 mins
    Description

    Director: Luca Guadagnino.
    Starring: Tilda Swinton, Ralph Fiennes, Matthias Schoenaerts, Dakota Johnson. Italy 2015. 124 mins.

    Marianne (Swinton), an ageing rock star, is enjoying an extended holiday on a Sicilian island, following career-threatening throat surgery, with filmmaker boyfriend Paul (Schoenaerts, Rust And Bone). Their peace is disrupted when Harry (Fiennes) – Marianne’s bombastic, avidly freethinking ex – and his precocious daughter, Penelope (Johnson), turn up to stay, uninvited. The ensuing friction, jealousy, passionate intrigue and fierce recriminations fuel Luca Guadagnino’s often funny, constantly edgy film inspired by Jacques Deray’s 1969 psychological drama La Piscine.

    Doyenne of immersive characterisation Swinton, who also fronted Guadagnino’s sumptuous I Am Love, brilliantly navigates the extremes provoked by this emotional roller coaster, largely without actually being able to speak due to her character’s recuperation. The remaining ensemble – especially Fiennes, exuding all the energetic intensity of his role in The Grand Budapest Hotel – supports her to perfection.

    More details

  • Friday 19th Feb

  • Bone Tomahawk [18]

    2016, mins
    Description

    Director: S. Craig Zahler.
    Starring: Kurt Russell, Patrick Wilson, Matthew Fox. USA 2015. 132 mins.

    A disparate posse led by sheriff Hunt (a heavily bearded Russell) sets out from a remote western town to save the town’s doctor, Samantha (Lili Simmons), who has been abducted by a tribe of cannibalistic cave dwellers known as Troglodytes.

    Writer and debut director S. Craig Zahler has crafted an entertainingly original and stylishly filmed hybrid of black comedy, western and horror. Patrick Wilson is Samantha’s aggrieved husband, Matthew Fox is a chippy gunslinger, and the fourth member of the intrepid group is Richard Jenkins as Hunt’s bumbling deputy.

    Much of the pleasure and certainly the humour lies in the quirky, discursive dialogue between this charismatic crew on their arduous ride as they journey towards the awfulness that awaits them. When they eventually reach the cave, they discover that the Trogoldytes have a particularly nasty way of dispatching their victims.

  • Freeheld [12A]

    2016, mins
    Description

    Director: Peter Sollett.
    Starring: Julianne Moore, Ellen Page, Michael Shannon. USA 2015. 103 mins.

    Inspired by the Oscar-winning 2007 documentary of the same name, Freeheld tells the ultimately uplifting tale of New Jersey cop Laurel Hester (Moore). Having kept her sexuality a closely guarded secret from her colleagues throughout her career, she finally finds love with Stacie (Page, Juno, Inception), only to be diagnosed with terminal cancer.

    Hoping to at least bequeath her pension to Stacie, so that she can afford to stay in the home they made together, Laurel petitions a board of county officials known as Freeholders. But they deny her claim because in their jurisdiction, this right isn’t available to same-sex couples. Backed by her steely detective partner (Shannon, 99 Homes) and a group of activists, Laurel then battles against both prejudice and precedent to achieve equality.

    Contains infrequent strong language and moderate sex references.

  • Goosebumps 2D [PG]

    2016, mins
    Description

    Director: Rob Letterman.
    Starring: Jack Black, Dylan Minnette, Odeya Rush. USA/Australia 2015. 103 mins.

    The reliably hilarious Jack Black teams up with Monsters Vs Aliens director Rob Letterman and co-writer Darren Limke (Jack The Giant Slayer) to conjure a beguiling adventure that melds live action with fantasy characters drawn from the bestselling teen novels of R. L. Stine. Black plays Stine himself, whom we find living in a small Delaware town with his teenage daughter Hannah (Rush). Then along comes young Zach (Minnette), a reluctant transplant from New York City, who soon becomes anxious to impress Hannah. Having discovered the identity of her curmudgeonly dad, Zach finds out the hard way that the monsters in Stine’s books are in fact real and not just the products of an author’s imagination. When the creatures are inadvertently unleashed upon the town, there follows a race against time to return them back between the pages.

  • Spotlight [15]

    2016, 129 mins
    Description

    Director: Tom McCarthy.
    Starring: Mark Ruffalo, Michael Keaton, Rachel McAdams, Liev Schreiber. USA 2015. 129 mins.

    The story of a group of journalists’ Pulitzer Prize-winning exposure of wrongdoing in the Catholic Church is brought to life by an ensemble of the best actors around.

    The Boston Globe’s so-called Spotlight team – ‘Robby’ Robinson (Keaton), Michael Rezendes (Ruffalo), Sacha Pfeiffer (McAdams) and Matty Carroll (Brian d’Arcy James) – specialises in deep investigative journalism. When they hear of allegations that Boston’s cardinal knew about, and covered up, child abuse by priests, they are incredulous at first. But their editor (Schreiber) quietly insists that they pursue it, and we see the profound effects on all concerned – at the Globe, and in the community at large – as the horrifying truth emerges.

    At a time when serious journalism seems under attack from all sides, Spotlight is an important as well as a gripping film: grown-up, serious but thrillingly entertaining.

    More details
